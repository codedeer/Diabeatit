What is Diabetes?

Diabetes slows down the body's ability to make and use insulin, a hormone that helps your body convert sugars from food into energy. If your body doesn't produce enough insulin, sugar builds up in your blood and make it harder for your heart to pump. There are two types of diabetes: Type 1 and Type 2.


Type 1 Diabetes is usually inherited from one's parents, and occurs when one's pancreas does not produce insulin. 5-10% of people affected with diabetes are diagnosed with Type 1 Diabetes.


Type 2 Diabetes is much more common. It occurs when the body can't produce enough insulin or it has become resistant to insulin; the body does not use insulin properly so there is still a build-up of sugar in the blood.
